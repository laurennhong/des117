Utilizing Semantic HTML

September 28, 2021

For my blog I am utilizing a basic but efficient structure. This structure will be easily readable by Googlebots and for readers. For my blog I have used semantic HTML tags such as header, nav, section, article, and footer. Using these tags, I section out the article well so readers can clearly see a visual hierarchy. To also help with the hierarchy, I used nested tags.

One element of the design that I believe encourages readers to continue reading is by adding a teaser of the article on the home page then adding a “read more” link that will take them to the rest of the article. This is a simple way to use semantic HTML to keep readers interested.

Some challenges I have experienced in visual design is not making websites seem too word-heavy. Sometimes it can get overwhelming with the amount of text that is in a blog. Readers also tend to have shorter attention spans, thus, having a screen full of words is not very enticing. A way to solve this is by separating articles into many paragraphs and to add images to your advantage. The semantic tags img and p help with this.

Overall, semantic tags are helpful for coders to better organize their code and for readers by making websites easier to read.
